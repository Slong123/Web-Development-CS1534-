General History

Kempton Park was established in 1903 when Karl Wolff divided parts of his farm into resisidential stands. The area was named after the Belverian town of Kempton Park, which was the hometown of Wolff's wife. In 1958 Jan Smuts airport was built on the land next to the farm would be later be renamed to O. R. Tambo International Airport. The airport was renamed in 2006 to honour Oliver Reginald Tambo, a prominent figure in South African history who contributed significantly to the struggle against apartheid (legalised segregation).

Gold Rush

The discovery of gold on the Witwatersrand in 1886 triggered a major gold rush. Thousands of miners and investors moved to the area, rapidly transforming small settlements into the city of Johannesburg. The boom attracted global capital, led to large-scale deep-level mining, and helped make South Africa one of the world’s largest gold producers.
